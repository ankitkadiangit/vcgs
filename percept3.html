Pre-test percept NIPT guide

A genetic test of you and your reproductive partner to look at what genes you may pass on to your children.

What conditions does percept NIPT look for?

Percept NIPT looks for conditions across all 23 pairs of chromosomes.

  • This includes the three most common chromosome conditions seen in pregnancy:
    • Down syndrome
    • Edwards syndrome and
    • Patau syndrome.
  • Other less common conditions caused by changes to the number of chromosomes.
  • Conditions caused by missing or extra pieces of chromosomes.
  • Sex chromosome conditions caused by changes to the number of X and Y chromosomes. Testing of these chromosomes can also identify the sex of your baby.

    Is NIPT right for me?

    Like all prenatal screening tests, NIPT is optional. Some women/couples want this information during their pregnancies. Others do not. It's a personal choice.

    Here are some points to help you think about your feelings and views on this test. It may help to discuss these with your partner (or doctor) before having screening.

    NIPT will not give you a yes or no answer. It will tell you if there is an increased chance that your pregnancy may have a chromosome condition.

    Is this type of information important and/or useful to you?

    NIPT can give false results. For example: False positive = NIPT says high chance, but your pregnancy does not have a condition. False negative = NIPT says low chance, but your pregnancy does have a condition.

    How would a false result make you feel?

    NIPT looks for a wide range of chromosome conditions. Some are rare. Sometimes the effect on health and development of the baby is uncertain.
