NIPT looks at your baby's chromosomes

NIPT is a genetic test of your pregnancy to estimate the chance a chromosome condition may be present.

What conditions does percept NIPT look for?

Percept NIPT looks for conditions across all 23 pairs of chromosomes.

  • This includes the three most common chromosome conditions seen in pregnancy:
    • Down syndrome
    • Edwards syndrome and
    • Patau syndrome.
  • Other less common conditions caused by changes to the number of chromosomes.
  • Conditions caused by missing or extra pieces of chromosomes.
  • Sex chromosome conditions caused by changes to the number of X and Y chromosomes. Testing of these chromosomes can also identify the sex of your baby.

A chromosome is a structure that carries the DNA we inherit from our parents. We have 23 pairs of chromosomes, or 46 in total. The last pair of chromosomes determines our sex. Males have an X and a Y chromosome pair, while females have two X chromosomes

What will percept NIPT tell me?

percept NIPT will tell you if there is a high or low chance that your pregnancy may have a chromosome condition.

Most results are reported as low chance. This means it is very unlikely that your pregnancy has any of the conditions screened. But, other conditions may still be present. There is no screening test that can check for all conditions.

Is NIPT right for me?

Like all prenatal screening tests, NIPT is optional. Some women/couples want this information during their pregnancies. Others do not. It's a personal choice.

Here are some points to help you think about your feelings and views on this test. It may help to discuss these with your partner (or doctor) before having screening.

NIPT will not give you a yes or no answer. It will tell you if there is an increased chance that your pregnancy may have a chromosome condition.

Is this type of information important and/or useful to you?

NIPT can give false results. For example: False positive = NIPT says high chance, but your pregnancy does not have a condition. False negative = NIPT says low chance, but your pregnancy does have a condition.

How would a false result make you feel?

NIPT looks for a wide range of chromosome conditions. Some are rare. Sometimes the effect on health and development of the baby is uncertain.

Would you be comfortable with this type of information?

Most people receive low chance NIPT results. This means there is a low chance of the pregnancy having a chromosome condition.

How would a low chance result make you feel?

Some people receive high/increased risk NIPT results. This means there is a chance the pregnancy may have a chromosome condition. In this case, diagnostic testing will be offered to clarify the NIPT result.

How would a high chance result make you feel?

If you receive a high chance NIPT result, you can choose to:

  • Continue the pregnancy with no further testing
  • Have a diagnostic test to find out for sure if the pregnancy has a chromosome condition.

Diagnostic testing requires an invasive CVS or amniocentesis procedure.

What might you do if you received a high chance NIPT result?

Some of the questions may be confronting. Our genetic counselling team is here to help you make a choice that is right for you.
