Carrier screening guide

A genetic test of you and your reproductive partner to look at what genes you may pass on to your children.

VCGS offers two carrier screening options

Prepair looks to see if you're a carrier for 3 conditions that are common in our population.

  • Cystic fibrosis (CF)
  • Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)
  • Fragile X syndrome (FXS)

90% of people identified as carriers of these conditions have no known family history of the condition.

Cystic fibrosis (CF)

It primarily affects the lungs and digestive system. Visit Cystic Fibrosis Australia for more information.

Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)

It affects the nerves in the spinal cord and causes muscles to get progressively weaker. Visit SMA Australia for more information.

Fragile X syndrome (FXS)

is the most common cause of inherited intellectual disability. Visit Fragile X Association of Australia for more information.

If you have a high chance, you can consider options like IVF and donor egg/sperm.

Carrier screening is relevant to everyone planning a pregnancy.

What will carrier screening tell me?

  • Carrier screening looks at whether you and your reproductive partner are carriers for a range of genetic conditions. This information tells you about your chance of having a child with an inherited genetic condition.
  • Most couples find out they have a low chance of having a child with an inherited genetic condition.
  • About 2% of couples will find out they have an increased chance of having a child with a genetic condition. In this case, our team will help you understand the results and the options available to you (such as IVF).
