Carrier screening looks at your genes

It's a genetic test of you and your reproductive partner to look at what genetic conditions you may pass on to your children.

VCGS offers two carrier screening options

prepair looks to see if you're a carrier for 3 conditions that are common in our population.

  • Cystic fibrosis (CF)
  • Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)
  • Fragile X syndrome (FXS)
CF

Cystic fibrosis (CF)

It primarily affects the lungs and digestive system. Visit Cystic Fibrosis Australia for more information.

FXS

Fragile X syndrome (FXS)

is the most common cause of inherited intellectual disability. Visit Fragile X Association of Australia for more information.

SMA

Spinal muscular atrophy (SMA)

It affects the nerves in the spinal cord and causes muscles to get progressively weaker. Visit SMA Australia for more information.

If you have a high chance, you can consider options like IVF and donor egg/sperm.

Carrier screening is relevant to everyone planning a pregnancy.

Did you know

About 1 in 20 individuals is a carrier for either CF, FXS or SMA

About 1 in 240 couples have an increased chance of having a child with either CF, SMA or FXS

9 out of 10 of carriers have no known family history

What will carrier screening tell me?

  • Carrier screening looks at whether you and your reproductive partner are carriers for a range of genetic conditions. This information tells you about your chance of having a child with an inherited genetic condition.
  • Most couples find out they have a low chance of having a child with an inherited genetic condition.
  • About 2% of couples have an increased chance of having a child with a genetic condition (see figure). In this case, our team will help you understand the results and the options available to you (such as testing a pregnancy, or IVF with genetic testing of embryos).

Is carrier screening right for me?

Choosing to have a genetic screening test is a personal choice and there are lots of things to consider. Here are some points to help you think about your feelings and views on this test. It may help to discuss these with your partner (or doctor) before having screening.

  • Carrier screening is optional. Is this something you want to do?
  • Do you want information about your chance of having a child with genetic condition?
  • Would you feel reassured by a result showing there was a low chance of having a child with a genetic condition?
  • How might you feel if your results show you have an increased chance of having a child with a genetic condition?
